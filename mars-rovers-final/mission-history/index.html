Mission History

Timeline of NASA’s Mars Rover Program

NASA’s Mars Rover Program has advanced scientific discovery by sending robotic explorers to study Mars’ surface, climate, and geology. Each mission builds on the one before, using improved technology to explore farther and operate longer than ever imagined.

January 4, 2004

Spirit Lands at Gusev Crater

Spirit was designed to analyze Martian rocks and terrain for evidence of ancient water. The rover far exceeded its 90-day mission, lasting more than six years. It discovered silica deposits that suggested Mars once had hot springs.

January 25, 2004

Opportunity Begins Its Journey

Opportunity landed in a region known to contain water-formed minerals. It discovered layered rocks and minerals that revealed Mars once had a wet environment capable of supporting life.

August 6, 2012

Curiosity Arrives in Gale Crater

Curiosity landed using the sky crane system and went on to discover ancient lakebeds, organic compounds, and radiation levels that help prepare humans for Mars missions.

Legacy of the Rovers

Together, Spirit, Opportunity, and Curiosity provided clear evidence that Mars once had liquid water and conditions that may have supported life.

These missions reshaped Mars science and set the foundation for future robotic and human exploration.
