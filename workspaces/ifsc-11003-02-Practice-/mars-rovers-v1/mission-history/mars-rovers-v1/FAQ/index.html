Answers About NASA’s Mars Rovers

Here are some commonly asked questions about Mars rovers and their missions.

General Questions

What is a Mars rover?
A Mars rover is a robotic vehicle designed to travel across the surface of Mars and study the planet’s environment, rocks, and atmosphere.

Why does NASA send rovers to Mars?
Rovers help scientists search for signs of ancient life, learn about Mars’ climate history, and prepare for future human missions.

How do rovers send data back to Earth?
Rovers transmit signals to Mars orbiters, which then relay information back to Earth using radio waves.

How long do rovers last?
Many rovers are designed to work for a few years, but some last much longer. Opportunity operated for nearly 15 years!

About the Rovers

What was the purpose of Spirit and Opportunity?
These twin rovers were built to search for signs of water on Mars and study rocks to understand the planet’s past.

What makes Curiosity different?
Curiosity is larger, nuclear-powered, and carries an advanced laboratory to analyze chemical samples.

Can rovers take selfies?
Yes! Rovers use special cameras to take photos of themselves, mostly to inspect equipment and wheels.

Do rovers move on their own?
Rovers can drive short distances autonomously, but engineers on Earth plan most of their movement.

Fun Facts

How fast do rovers drive?
Very slowly — about the speed of a turtle (around 0.1 mph).

What is the largest rover?
Curiosity is one of the largest rovers ever sent to Mars.

Can rovers get stuck?
Yes — Spirit got trapped in soft soil in 2009 and could not escape.
